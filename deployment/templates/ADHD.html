You are diagnosed with ADHD

Definition

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning and development.

Causes

  • Genetics: ADHD tends to run in families, suggesting a genetic component.
  • Brain function and structure: Differences in brain anatomy and activity have been observed in individuals with ADHD.
  • Environmental factors: Exposure to toxins, maternal smoking during pregnancy, premature birth, and low birth weight may contribute to the development of ADHD.

Symptoms

  • Inattention: Difficulty sustaining attention, easily distracted, forgetfulness, and difficulty organizing tasks.
  • Hyperactivity: Restlessness, fidgeting, excessive talking, and difficulty staying seated.
  • Impulsivity: Acting without thinking, interrupting others, and difficulty waiting for turns.

Treatment

  • Behavioral therapy: Behavioral interventions, such as psychoeducation, skill-building, and behavior modification techniques, can help manage ADHD symptoms and improve functioning.
  • Medication: Stimulant medications, such as methylphenidate or amphetamines, are commonly prescribed to reduce symptoms of ADHD.
  • Parenting and education support: Providing support and resources to parents and educators can help create a structured and supportive environment for individuals with ADHD.
