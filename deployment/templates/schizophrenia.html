You are diagnosed with Schizophrenia

Definition

Schizophrenia is a complex mental health disorder characterized by a disconnection from reality, abnormal thinking patterns, and altered perceptions of oneself and the world.

Causes

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Imbalances in brain chemicals and neurotransmitters
  • Environmental factors, such as exposure to viruses or prenatal complications
  • Stressful life events

Symptoms

  • Delusions or false beliefs
  • Hallucinations, often auditory or visual
  • Disorganized speech and thoughts
  • Difficulty with concentration and memory
  • Social withdrawal and reduced emotional expression

Treatment

  • Antipsychotic medications to manage symptoms
  • Psychosocial interventions, such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) or family therapy
  • Supportive services, including vocational rehabilitation and housing assistance
  • Self-help and coping strategies
