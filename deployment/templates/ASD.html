You are diagnosed with ASD

Definition

ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent challenges in social interaction, communication, and restricted and repetitive behaviors.

Causes

  • Genetic factors: Certain genetic mutations and variants are associated with an increased risk of ASD.
  • Environmental factors: Prenatal and early-life environmental influences, such as maternal infections, complications during pregnancy or delivery, and exposure to certain chemicals, may contribute to the development of ASD.
  • Neurological factors: Differences in brain structure and function have been observed in individuals with ASD.

Symptoms

  • Challenges in social interaction: Difficulty with nonverbal communication, forming and maintaining relationships, and understanding social cues.
  • Communication difficulties: Delayed or atypical language development, challenges in initiating and sustaining conversations, and reliance on repetitive or scripted language.
  • Restricted and repetitive behaviors: Engaging in repetitive movements or behaviors, rigid adherence to routines, and intense interests in specific topics.

Treatment

  • Behavioral interventions: Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) and other behavioral therapies can help individuals with ASD acquire new skills and reduce challenging behaviors.
  • Speech and language therapy: Targeted interventions can assist with improving communication skills and language development.
  • Occupational therapy: Occupational therapists can support individuals with ASD in developing adaptive skills and addressing sensory sensitivities.
  • Medication: In some cases, medication may be prescribed to manage specific symptoms or co-occurring conditions, such as anxiety or attention difficulties.
