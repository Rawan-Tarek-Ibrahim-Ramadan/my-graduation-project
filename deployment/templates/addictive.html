You are diagnosed with Additive Disorder

Definition

Additive Disorder is a mental health condition characterized by significant disturbances in a person's ability to control and regulate their use of substances or behaviors.

Causes

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Environmental factors and social influences
  • Psychological factors, such as stress or trauma
  • Co-occurring mental health disorders

Symptoms

  • Compulsive and excessive engagement in substance use or addictive behaviors
  • Difficulty in cutting down or controlling substance use or behaviors
  • Neglecting personal and professional responsibilities due to addiction
  • Withdrawal symptoms and cravings when attempting to stop or reduce substance use

Treatment

  • Psychotherapy, such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) or Motivational Interviewing
  • Medication-assisted treatment, if applicable
  • Support groups or 12-step programs
  • Behavioral interventions and coping strategies
