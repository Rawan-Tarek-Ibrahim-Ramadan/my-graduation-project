You are diagnosed with Trauma and Stressor-Related Disorder

Definition

Trauma and Stressor-Related Disorder is a mental health condition characterized by the development of emotional and behavioral symptoms following exposure to a traumatic or stressful event.

Causes

  • Experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event
  • Chronic or severe stress
  • Childhood adversity or abuse
  • Genetic and biological factors

Symptoms

  • Intrusive memories or flashbacks of the traumatic event
  • Avoidance of reminders or triggers associated with the trauma
  • Negative changes in mood and cognition
  • Hyperarousal, including heightened startle response and difficulty sleeping
  • Impaired social and occupational functioning

Treatment

  • Psychotherapy, such as Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT) or Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR)
  • Medications, such as selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), to manage symptoms
  • Supportive counseling and therapy
  • Self-care practices, including stress management techniques and relaxation exercises
