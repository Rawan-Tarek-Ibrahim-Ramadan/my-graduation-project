You are diagnosed with Panic Disorder

Definition

Panic Disorder is a mental health condition characterized by recurrent and unexpected panic attacks, which are intense periods of fear or discomfort that reach their peak within minutes. Panic attacks are often accompanied by physical symptoms and a sense of impending doom.

Causes

  • Genetic predisposition
  • Abnormalities in brain areas responsible for fear and anxiety responses
  • Major life stressors
  • History of childhood trauma or abuse

Symptoms

  • Recurrent and unexpected panic attacks
  • Palpitations, pounding heart, or accelerated heart rate
  • Sweating
  • Trembling or shaking
  • Shortness of breath or feeling of being smothered

Treatment

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) to identify and modify negative thought patterns and behaviors associated with panic attacks
  • Medications, such as selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), to reduce the frequency and severity of panic attacks
  • Relaxation techniques, including deep breathing exercises and progressive muscle relaxation
  • Lifestyle changes, such as regular exercise, adequate sleep, and stress management
