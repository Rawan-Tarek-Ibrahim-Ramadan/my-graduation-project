You are diagnosed with Bipolar Disorder

Definition

Bipolar Disorder is a mental health condition characterized by extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).

Causes

  • Genetics: Bipolar Disorder tends to run in families, indicating a genetic predisposition.
  • Brain chemistry and structure: Imbalances in brain chemicals and abnormalities in brain structure are associated with Bipolar Disorder.
  • Environmental factors: Stressful life events, substance abuse, and certain medications may trigger or worsen symptoms of Bipolar Disorder.

Symptoms

  • Manic symptoms: Elevated mood, increased energy, impulsivity, racing thoughts, and decreased need for sleep.
  • Depressive symptoms: Sadness, loss of interest, fatigue, changes in appetite, and thoughts of death or suicide.

Treatment

  • Medication: Mood stabilizers, antipsychotics, and antidepressants are commonly prescribed to manage Bipolar Disorder symptoms.
  • Therapy: Psychotherapy, such as cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) or interpersonal therapy (IPT), can help individuals cope with mood swings, manage stress, and improve relationships.
  • Lifestyle changes: Maintaining a regular sleep schedule, managing stress, avoiding alcohol and drugs, and engaging in regular exercise can support mood stability.
